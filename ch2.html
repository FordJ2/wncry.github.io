Part Description
CPU CPU stands for Central Processing Unit. A CPU is the brain of a computer, and CPUs can do anything from basic word processing to intense 3D rendering. While most modern CPUs require an external Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) for a display output, some older CPUs have integrated graphics which makes the CPU more independent.
Heatsink Due to high heat output from the electricity in parts of the computer, a heat dispersion tool is vital for the survival of the computer. A heat sink, most often found on a CPU or GPU, helps move heat away from its source, and into a place where it can be easily cooled.
Fans Fans are designed to cool down computer components efficiently. Some fans are designed for ultimate silence for those who are sensitive and can also be designed as a source of decorative lights inside a computer.
Motherboard The motherboard is the central piece of the system that connects all pieces of a computer together. It also monitors the temperature of the different components.
RAM Random Access Memory (RAM) is the short term storage in a computer. All temporary, data such as layers in photoshop or tabs in a browser, are stored on RAM cards.
Hard Drive While RAM deals with short term memory, hard drives deal with long term storage. Files and programs are stored on hard drives.
Expansion Slots Expansion slots are slots designed to support cards that can expand your motherboard. Whether it be graphics (GPU), sound, networking or even storage, expansion cards and slots are invaluable in a system.
PSU PSU's (power supply units) are the heart of a computer, and supply power to all internal components.
Data Cables Data cables are responsible for the transference of data from the motherboard to storage devices.
Power cables Power cables are responsible delivering power from the power supply to all the internal components.
BIOS Chips BIOS chips manage the computer startup sequences. It can provide information about errors in your system, as well as help you manage your components in the computer.
CD-ROM Drives CD-ROM drives are input devices that can read DVDs, CD-ROM discs and CDs. Most forms of media have moved online, making CD-ROM drives pretty much entirely useless
Floppy Disk Drives Floppy disk drives are ports that accept a format of storage called floppy discs. While not present on most modern systems, they were vital in their era.
